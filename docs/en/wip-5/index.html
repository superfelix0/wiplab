US Liquidity Monitor

Combines M2, reserve balances, reverse repo, and the Treasury General Account to read U.S. liquidity.

What to read

  • M2 tracks broad macro liquidity, while reserves, RRP, and TGA help estimate market-facing liquidity pressure.
  • Falling RRP and TGA with rising reserves are generally more supportive for market liquidity.
  • Liquidity is a directional backdrop and should be read with rates, earnings, and policy events.

Liquidity dashboard

Recent changes over roughly three months. Rising M2 and reserves are supportive; rising RRP and TGA can absorb liquidity.
